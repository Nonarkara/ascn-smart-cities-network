## Key facts extracted so far

Framework (`ascn-framework-2018.pdf`):
- Endorsed by ASCN on 8 July 2018.
- Defines ASCN as a non-binding guide for city-specific smart city development.
- Frames three strategic objectives around competitive economy, sustainable environment, and high quality of life.
- Emphasizes technology and non-technological innovation, with a people-centred approach.

Concept note (`ascn-concept-note.pdf`):
- Positions ASCN as a collaborative platform for up to three cities per ASEAN Member State at launch, with room for expansion.
- Sets three core aims: facilitate cooperation, catalyse bankable projects, and secure external partner support.
- Ties city action plans to the 2018-2025 horizon aligned with ASEAN Community Vision 2025.

Monitoring and Evaluation report 2025 (`ascn-monitoring-evaluation-2025.pdf`):
- Covers October 2024 to September 2025.
- Reports 134 total smart city projects as of September 2025.
- Reports project status split of 108 ongoing, 18 completed, and 8 in planning.
- Organises projects across six focus areas:
  - Civic and Social
  - Health and Well-Being
  - Safety and Security
  - Quality Environment
  - Built Infrastructure
  - Industry and Innovation

Planning guidebook (`asean-smart-city-planning-guidebook-2022.pdf`):
- Prepared by Japan MLIT in consultation with ASCN and the ASEAN Secretariat.
- Structured around fundamentals, priority areas, and process/enablers for smart city development.
- Useful as a content source for methodology, taxonomy, and roadmap UX.

Contact list (`ascn-contact-list-2026-01-31.pdf`):
- Dated 31 January 2026.
- Contains National Representatives and Chief Smart City Officers for current member cities.
- Useful as a current membership roster and city list validation source.

East Asia Summit statement (`eas-leaders-statement-asean-smart-cities-2018.pdf`):
- Adopted on 15 November 2018 at the 13th East Asia Summit in Singapore.
- Reaffirms support for ASCN, city-to-city interaction, partner mobilisation, innovation, and inclusive development.

## Why the current page feels weak

- It behaves like an archive page, not a living network hub.
- It does not surface city-level momentum, project progress, or cross-city comparison.
- It hides the most interesting material inside PDFs.
- It has no live data, no newsroom layer, no dashboards, and no narrative about current relevance.
- It does not foreground member cities, officials, partnerships, or outcomes.

## Strong redesign directions

- Make the landing page a live ASCN dashboard, not a static repository.
- Surface the 38 member cities immediately with interactive filtering by country, focus area, and growth stage.
- Turn the M&E reports into visible metrics:
  - total projects
  - ongoing vs completed vs planning
  - focus-area distribution
  - growth over time
- Add city scorecards or trend cards driven by a structured dataset derived from the M&E reports and SCAPs.
- Add a Southeast Asia smart cities news layer fed by curated APIs and RSS sources.
- Add a section for current chairmanship, upcoming meetings, partner initiatives, and featured city stories.
- Use the framework and guidebook as the conceptual backbone, but present them as accessible visual systems instead of buried PDFs.

## Recommended next research tasks

- Extract a structured city roster from the contact list and SCAPs.
- Build a machine-readable dataset from the 2022-2025 M&E reports.
- Define a lightweight scoring model for "smart city momentum" that is transparent and defensible.
- Inventory external news/data sources for Southeast Asian smart city signals.
- Collect photos, maps, and city visuals for a more ambitious landing page.
